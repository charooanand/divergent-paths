Divergent Paths

A New Perspective On Earnings Differences Between Black And White Men Since 1940

The second half of the 20th century began with progressivist hopes for racial equality – or at least a march towards it. In particular, the 1950s and '60s ushered in landmark gains for African Americans in the form of desegregation, Civil Rights and Voting Rights.
But did legislative gains translate to economic advancement? Bayer & Charles (2018) revise current understanding on the Black-White earnings gaps in the US. By looking beyond simple differences in average incomes, their analysis paints a richer picture of income inequality.

Let's explore two different ways to visualize differences in Black and White men's earnings.

The Earnings Level Gap

If we're thinking about how much more White men earned compared to their Black counterparts, a natural measure is to compare the median income of a White man to that of a Black man. This is a simple and informative statistic. However, the median tells us about the experience of very specific people – two men who are right in the middle of their group's income distribution. What about two men who are top earners? Or two men at the 15th percentile? Those comparisons are also informative.

The Earnings Rank Gap

Another way to measure differences is to ask: where would a Black man's earnings place him within the White income distribution? For example, if the median Black man earned $30k, that puts him at the 50th percentile among Black men, but maybe only the 20th percentile among White men — a 30-point gap.
